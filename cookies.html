Cookie Policy

Last Updated: June 13, 2026

This is the official Cookie Policy for IconCopy. We believe in complete transparency about how we handle user data. This document explains what cookies and web storage mechanisms are used on our platform, why they are necessary, and how you can manage them.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files downloaded to your device by your web browser when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites function efficiently and remember your preferences. In addition to cookies, modern web utility tools use LocalStorage, which saves data directly inside your browser container without expiring automatically or sending data packets over the network.

2. First-Party Technical Storage (LocalStorage)

IconCopy inherently does not deploy native tracking cookies on its own server infrastructure. Instead, our application utilizes your browser's sandboxed LocalStorage to deliver basic utility features. These elements are non-intrusive and include:

  • User Interface Layout: To save your preferred visual styling selection, such as switching between Light Mode and Dark Mode configurations. This ensures the site displays correctly on your next session.
  • Clipboard History: A localized array that temporarily caches your recently copied emojis or icons, allowing you to instantly access your favorite items without re-searching. This data remains 100% stored on your local device hardware.

3. Third-Party Cookie Deployments (Google AdSense)

When you browse IconCopy, external automated network vendors may deploy third-party advertising cookies to manage and measure the distribution of marketing campaigns. This is required for platform operations:

  • Google AdSense Infrastructure: Google, as a third-party advertisement vendor, uses specialized tracking tags known as DART Cookies. These scripts analyze aggregate browsing signals across IconCopy and other portals on the internet to deliver tailored, relevant commercial ads to you.
  • Frequency Capping: These third-party cookies track whether an ad has already been rendered to your screen, preventing the system from spamming you with the same identical advertisement multiple times.

4. How to Control and Disable Cookies

You have the absolute structural right to accept, limit, or completely block cookies. You can manage your choices by modifying your individual web browser preferences. Please note that restricting cookies may disable certain interface elements or cause formatting layout inconsistencies on IconCopy.

To disable cookies manually, please consult the documentation provided by your web browser developer:

  • Google Chrome Settings Guide
  • Mozilla Firefox Privacy Guide
  • Apple Safari Cookies Guide
  • Microsoft Edge Data Settings

To permanently opt out of Google's interest-based behavioral profiling cookies, you can visit the official Google Advertising Privacy center directly: https://policies.google.com/technologies/ads
